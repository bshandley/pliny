# Pliny Pricing Strategy
**North Star Document**

## The Vision
Be so cheap it's stupid not to try. Be so good they stay.

## Core Pricing Model

### Free (Self-Hosted)
- **Cost:** $0 forever
- **Features:** Full product, no limits
- **Users:** Unlimited
- **Boards/Cards:** Unlimited
- **Support:** Community (GitHub issues)
- **Why:** Adoption engine, trust builder, open source goodwill

### Managed Hosting: $10/month
- **Cost:** Flat $10/month for the whole team
- **Users:** Unlimited
- **Features:** Everything (no tiers, no upsells)
- **Support:** Email support
- **Backups:** Daily automated backups
- **Uptime:** 99.5% SLA
- **Why:** Remove all friction, make it a no-brainer

### Enterprise (Future)
- **Cost:** $100/month (only when needed)
- **Includes:** Dedicated instance, 99.9% SLA, SSO/SAML, priority support
- **When:** Only offer this when customers ask for it
- **Why:** Capture large orgs that need compliance

## Differentiation

### Why Pliny Wins
1. **Actually affordable** - Not "affordable per seat," actually affordable
2. **Actually open source** - Not fake "source available" license tricks
3. **No artificial limits** - Free tier isn't crippled to force upgrades
4. **Clean, fast UX** - Dark mode, mobile-responsive, no bloat
5. **Privacy-first** - Self-hostable, no data mining

### What We Don't Compete On
- Enterprise features (SSO, SAML, audit logs) - those come later
- Integrations - start simple, add as we grow
- White-label - not needed for MVP
- Advanced automations - keep it simple

## Principles

1. **Pricing is a feature** - The $10 flat rate is a differentiator
2. **Simplicity wins** - No tiers, no seat counting, no tricks
3. **Open source first** - Never compromise on this
4. **Profit per team, not per user** - Volume over margin
5. **No growth-hacking** - Organic, sustainable growth only
6. **Community over customers** - They're people, not ATMs

## Red Lines (Never Do This)

❌ Add per-user pricing  
❌ Cripple free tier to force upgrades  
❌ Sell user data  
❌ Pivot to "source available" licensing  
❌ Add dark patterns (fake urgency, hidden costs)  
❌ Raise prices for existing customers  
❌ Lock features behind paywalls mid-stream  

## FAQ (Internal)

**Q: What if people abuse the $10 unlimited?**  
A: Worry about that when it happens. Most teams are 5-20 people. A 200-person team paying $10/mo is still good marketing.

**Q: Why not charge more?**  
A: Volume play. 1,000 teams @ $10 = 100 teams @ $100. Easier to get 1,000 teams.

**Q: What about costs at scale?**  
A: $10/mo easily covers hosting for most teams. Hetzner/DO keeps costs low. Break-even at ~$5-7/team, profit $3-5/team.

**Q: How do we compete with free (Trello, others)?**  
A: We ARE free (self-hosted). We're also $10/mo managed vs Trello's $50+/mo for paid tier.

**Q: What if competitors undercut us?**  
A: They won't. $10/mo unlimited is already a race to the bottom. We're fine there.
